    Artist Statement

    I explore the construction of identity, and challenge the traditional role of the viewer through painting and drawing. Manipulation of texture and form creates an image that oscillates between flat and deep, human and inhuman. The unique reality of the image encourages viewers to engage with the sitter intimately, disregarding social norms and habits. The sitter is simultaneously idiosyncratic and innately relatable. In confronting the sitter, the viewer confronts his or herself.
