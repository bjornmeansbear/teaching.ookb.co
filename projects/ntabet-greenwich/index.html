THE
GREENWICH
PROJECT
creates
72dpi relations
Acquiring a training in graphic design at often times involves a methodological process that is highly sensitive to surroundings. Our perception of minute details around us is amplified and affects how we creatively inform our work.

The Greenwich Project is a digital platform that examines the cultural ramifications of immigrating away from home in pursuit of an international design exposure, whether professional or educational. The project is a varied set of collaborations through 72 dpi exchanges, between several Lebanese graphic designers across different time zones.

Every designer's input is based on detailed observations and recorded materials that dictate the rhythm of his or her creative process in the local culture despite globalization.
